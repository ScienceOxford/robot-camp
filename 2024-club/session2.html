understanding your robot power board

The board you are going to assemble today controls the power to different parts of the robot.

Your robot will be powered with a 9V battery, but this is too high a voltage for the micro:bit (which needs 3.3V), and for the ultrasonic sensors (which need 5V).

This board uses voltage regulators to reduce the voltage provided by the battery in different ways, giving you options for how to connect different components.

It is a bit like having three circuits connected together, and we are using different colours to indicate how much voltage each circuit uses.

  • Red = ~9V

    anything plugged in here uses the maximum voltage given by the batteries, used for the motors

  • Blue = 5V

    used for the ultrasonic distance sensors

  • Yellow = 3.3V

    used for the micro:bit

As you solder your board, pay attention to the colours of the components you are adding, to make sure they go in the correct section. This will make using and troubleshooting your board much easier in the future.

The picture below shows the components you will be adding, and what colour-coded section of the board they apply to.

  • Red ~9V Section

    • Battery connector
    • Set of red pin headers, so you can connect wires to the ~9V supply
    • Red LED, to give a visual reference that the battery is working
    • 470Ω resistor, to make sure the red LED works correctly

  • Blue 5V Section

    • 5V voltage regulator, to provide a consistent 5V supply to this section
    • Set of blue pin headers, so you can connect wires to the 5V supply
    • Blue LED, to give a visual reference that the 5V regulator is working
    • 100Ω resistor, to make sure the blue LED works correctly

  • Yellow 3.3V Section

    • 3.3V voltage regulator, to provide a consistent 3.3V supply to this section
    • Set of yellow pin headers, so you can connect wires to the 3.3V supply
    • Yellow LED, to give a visual reference that the 3.3V regulator is working
    • 68Ω resistor, to make sure the yellow LED works correctly

  • Other Components

    • Switch, so you can turn the board on and off
    • Capacitor, to help keep things running smoothly when your robot is drawing lots of power
    • Set of black pin headers, so you can connect wires to ground (negative)
